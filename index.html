Connect Your Own Backend

Run Taskwell on your own free Supabase project — your data stays fully separate from anyone else's.

You'll keep using this exact web address (jessieebie.github.io/taskwell/) — there's no separate website to visit. Below, you'll create a free Supabase project and paste its Project ID into a field here; you'll never open that Supabase project's address in a browser tab yourself.

Takes about 15 minutes, start to finish.

1 Create a free Supabase project

Go to supabase.com and create a project. Leave Enable Data API, Automatically expose new tables, and Enable automatic RLS at their defaults — the script in Step 2 already creates your tables and sets up security, so there's nothing extra to configure here.

4 Set your URL configuration Required

Click the lock icon (Authentication) in the left sidebar → URL Configuration under CONFIGURATION — not "Templates" or "Sign In / Providers."

  1. Set Site URL to https://jessieebie.github.io/taskwell/ and click Save changes.
  2. Under Redirect URLs, click Add URL, paste the same address, and save.

Without both of these, sign-in links redirect to localhost:3000 and fail.

5 Grab your Project ID & API key
  1. Project ID: look at your browser's address bar while viewing this project's dashboard — it's the random string right after /project/ in the URL (e.g. abcdefghijklmnop in supabase.com/dashboard/project/abcdefghijklmnop).
  2. API key: click the gear icon (Project Settings) in the left sidebar → API Keys. You'll see tabs at the top — use whichever one you have:
    • Tab says "Publishable and secret API keys" → copy the key under Publishable key.
    • Tab says "Legacy anon, service_role API keys" → copy the anon public key.
    Either way, never copy the "secret" or "service_role" key.

You'll paste both into the fields at the bottom of this page.

6 Set up Google Sign-In Required

You'll need this anyway if you want Calendar features later. Involves creating a free Google Cloud project — go slowly, it's hard to get wrong:

  1. Go to console.cloud.google.com. If you don't already have a project, click the Select a project button near the top, then New Project in the dialog that opens, name it anything (e.g. "Taskwell"), and click Create. Wait a few seconds, then click that same button again and make sure the new project is selected.
  2. In the search bar at the top, type Google Auth Platform and click it. If you see "Get Started," click it.
  3. App Information page: enter an app name (e.g. "Taskwell") and your own email as the support email, then click Next.
  4. Audience page: choose External, then click Next.
  5. Contact Information page: enter your email again, then click Next.
  6. Check the box agreeing to the policy, click Continue, then click Create.
  7. On the left, click Audience. Under Test users, click Add users, enter your own email (and anyone else you want to allow while testing), then Save. Without this, sign-in fails with "access blocked" for anyone not on this list — you can add up to 100 people, or "publish" the app later to remove this limit.
  8. On the left, click Clients, then Create Client. Application type: Web application, give it any name.
  9. Under Authorized redirect URIs, click Add URI and paste: https://YOUR-PROJECT-ID.supabase.co/auth/v1/callback (replace YOUR-PROJECT-ID with your Project ID from Step 5), then click Create.
  10. Your Client ID and Client Secret appear immediately — copy both somewhere safe now. The secret is only shown this once.
  11. Back in Supabase, go to Authentication → Sign In / Providers, scroll to Auth Providers, click Google, toggle it on, paste in your Client ID and Client Secret, and click Save.

Note: this Google setup covers signing in only. Calendar features (Connect Google Calendar, Outlook/Power Automate) need additional setup beyond this wizard.

ICS Feeds — view-only calendars

View-only. Paste a calendar's iCal/webcal link to see its events inside Taskwell. You can view these events, but Taskwell can't create, edit, or delete them. For that, connect Google Calendar below.

Get the link from Google Calendar: on a computer, open Google Calendar → hover the calendar under “My calendars” → ⋮ → Settings and sharing → scroll to “Secret address in iCal format” and copy that link.

Get the link from Outlook: open Outlook on the web → Settings → Calendar → Shared calendars → under Publish a calendar, pick the calendar, publish it, and copy the ICS link.

Google Calendar

Only needed if you want Taskwell to add or edit events on your Google Calendar. If you just want to see a Google calendar, add it as an ICS feed above instead — connecting isn't required.

To connect: click Connect Google Calendar, choose your Google account, and tap Allow on the permission screen. That's it — your events will start showing up.

Using a different account? Once connected, the box shows which account is linked. To switch, click Reconnect / switch account and pick the other one.

Birthdays

Google can surface contacts' birthdays as all-day events on your calendar. Choose whether Taskwell shows those or filters them out.

Second calendar account

Optional — add a second Google Calendar and say whether it's for Work or Personal events. Once saved, it shows up as a choice when creating a new event, and its events sync alongside your main calendar. Find the Calendar ID in Google Calendar under Settings → [that calendar] → Integrate calendar.

Add to a Managed Outlook Calendar

For work or school Outlook calendars you can't connect to directly. Enter the email address your Power Automate flow watches.

Power Automate is a Microsoft tool included with most work and school Microsoft 365 accounts — so if you have Outlook through work or school, you probably already have access to it (check at make.powerautomate.com).

How it works: when you create a new Work event, Taskwell emails it from your connected Google account with a subject starting #AddToCalendar. Your Power Automate flow sees that email and adds the event to your Outlook calendar automatically.

Switching between Work & Home

Taskwell keeps two separate spaces — one for Work, one for Home — plus a combined view. Switch between them with the buttons at the top of the sidebar.

✓
All (the Taskwell ✓ mark) — shows your Work and Home lists together in one combined view.
Work — only your work lists and tasks. The app switches to your Work color.
Home — only your home lists and tasks, in your Home color.

Your choice here carries through the whole app — Hub, Week, and Day all show whatever space you've picked.

The tabs

Below the Work/Home buttons are the views. They all respect the Work/Home/All choice above.

Hub — your lists, grouped into sections. This is where you add lists and tasks, and rename or delete sections.
Week — a 7-day board. Drag a task onto a day to plan out your week.
Day — today at a glance: your agenda, a mini calendar, and any connected calendar events.
Inbox — quick capture. Jot something down now and sort it into a list later.
Settings — calendars, colors, your account, this guide, and support. Sign out is the arrow at the very bottom of the sidebar.
